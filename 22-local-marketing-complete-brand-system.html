Strategy

22 Local Marketing should feel like an operating partner for serious home service businesses. The brand is not cute, trendy, or agency-fluffy. It is confident, useful, and revenue-aware.

Positioning

Local growth systems

Websites, local SEO, and paid acquisition for home service companies that want more qualified calls and booked jobs.

Audience

Owner-operators

Trades business owners who care about revenue, phone calls, reviews, service area visibility, and practical marketing.

Personality

Sharp, direct, grounded

Confident without being slick. Modern without looking like generic SaaS. Useful before decorative.

Identity

The logo is intentionally simple: a Blaze rounded square and a Sora wordmark. The square acts as a small signal of the system. Do not add icons, pins, initials, gradients, or extra marks inside it.

Voice

The voice should sound like a sharp operator, not a motivational marketer. Say what matters. Tie claims to outcomes. Keep copy concrete.

Say

More qualified calls.

Use tangible business outcomes: calls, booked jobs, reviews, rankings, revenue, service areas.

Avoid

Vague agency claims.

Avoid empty language like game-changing, full-service solutions, next-level growth, or cutting-edge strategies.

CTA

Make the next step clear.

Use direct CTAs: Book a call, Get a local audit, See the system, Build your growth engine.

Applications

The brand should be recognizable across social posts, ads, proposals, sales one-pagers, websites, reports, and business cards. Use orange frames, crisp type, and quiet structure.
